Department of Japanese Studies, University of Dhaka
at a glance

The Department of Japanese Studies at Dhaka University is a part of the faculty of Arts and is one of the leading centers for the study of Japanese language and culture in Bangladesh. It is an integral part of the University's mission to provide students with a well-rounded education that prepares them for success in the 21st century. The department has a rich history and has been providing students with high-quality education in Japanese language and culture for many years. In addition to language classes, the department also offers courses in Japanese literature, culture, and history. These courses provide students with a deeper understanding of Japan and its people, and help them to appreciate the unique cultural and historical contributions that Japan has made to the world. The department also places a strong emphasis on practical skills, such as the ability to speak, read, and write Japanese. The faculty members are experienced in teaching the language and use a variety of techniques and tools to help students improve their speaking and listening skills. The department also provides students with opportunities to participate in language exchange programs, which allow them to practice their Japanese with native speakers and gain valuable cultural experience. In addition to language and culture courses, the Department of Japanese Studies at Dhaka University also offers students a range of extracurricular activities and clubs. These activities include cultural events, guest lectures, and trips to Japan, which provide students with opportunities to deepen their understanding of Japanese culture and to make new friends. Overall, the Department of Japanese Studies at Dhaka University provides students with a comprehensive education in Japanese language and culture that prepares them for a wide range of careers and opportunities. With its strong commitment to research, innovation, and student success, the department is well positioned to continue to lead the way in the study of Japanese language and culture in Bangladesh.
